On Wednesday, May 6th, the good people who run https://dv.dataverse.lv will be giving a talk about their first year of serving their research community in Latvia. Gustavo and I will be there in Riga, in person, to present as well.

If you'd like to join, you can register for the event here: https://events.teams.microsoft.com/event/f11272b8-af13-4f3e-9ff1-f6bae3e19a17@695e0a27-5814-44f5-a5bd-d4dc3e8c3a47

Philip Durbin 🚀 (Apr 29 2026 at 19:59):

Here's the description from the link above:

"DataverseLV – Celebrating the First Year in the Dataverse community

Join us for a special event celebrating the first year of the DataverseLV research data repository and its growing role within the international Dataverse community.

This event offers a unique opportunity to look back on the achievements of DataverseLV during its first year, explore its impact on research data management in Latvia, and learn about future developments. Kristaps Oškalns and Daina Kosīte (VPC) will share insights into the repository’s journey, milestones, and vision ahead.

We are also delighted to welcome representatives from The Dataverse Project at Harvard University. Sonia Barbosa, Gustavo Durand and Philip Durbin will introduce the Dataverse software and provide a closer look at the vibrant global community that supports and advances open research data sharing.

Whether you are a researcher, data steward, librarian, or simply interested in research data infrastructure, this event is an excellent chance to connect, learn, and become part of the wider Dataverse network.

Programme

  • One year of DataverseLV research data repository in Latvia
    Kristaps Oškalns, Daina Kosīte (VPC)

  • Meeting the Dataverse Project team: overview of the Dataverse software and repository community worldwide
    Sonia Barbosa, Gustavo Durand, Philip Durbin (The Dataverse Project, Harvard University)

Location: LU Sapere Aude zālē, Kalpaka bulvāris 4, Rīga and online (Teams)

We warmly invite you to join us and learn about the latest advances in open research data sharing!"

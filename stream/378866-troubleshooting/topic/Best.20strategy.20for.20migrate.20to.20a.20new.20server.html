Oliver Bertuch (Mar 11 2026 at 16:09):

Once you installed your new server's operating system, it shouldn't be that hard to migrate the installation. It will require planned downtime though.

Here's some things you'll need to consider (not necessarily complete):

  • Shutdown the application, database and index before attempting any copy operations.
  • Depending on where you store your data at rest, you may need to copy the data from old to new. You're taking the easy way when you store your data on S3 or some network file system. For local filesystems, you will need a copy & sync job, probably with rsync. You could also start by only migrating your data to a new network filesystem in case you have it now on a local disk, that will make the transition easier.
  • Depending on how you installed Postgres and Solr, you can probably just use rsync to copy your installation's files (minus the actual data) from the old to the new server. When you used some package to install Postgres or Solr, you'll need to install them in the new location and then do a data migration with those. Not to hard, but you should read up on that.
  • Take care of any Cronjobs and Startup Scripts. Depending on your current OS you might need to adapt (e.g. because of SystemD changes). Cronjobs are usually easy to copy and paste between servers.

I can only highly recommend to automate such tasks to make it easier in case of necessary recovery.
Probably @Don Sizemore has more thoughts on this.

Oliver Bertuch (Mar 11 2026 at 16:09):

Also - what Phil said. Don't make a migration and an upgrade at the same time. Avoid too many moving parts at once.
